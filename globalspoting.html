Global Acquisition Strategies

Proven methods to identify and approach international businesses

Niche Communities

Target specialized groups

  • Facebook groups for specific industries
  • Subreddits for small business owners
  • LinkedIn industry groups
  • Discord/Slack communities

Example Targets:

"Small Business Owners USA" Facebook group
"/r/Entrepreneur" subreddit
"Etsy Sellers Worldwide" LinkedIn group

Freelancer Networks

Reach solopreneurs globally

  • Upwork/Fiverr service providers
  • Creative portfolios on Behance/Dribbble
  • Authors on Amazon Kindle Direct
  • Coaches on Teachable/Kajabi

Example Pitch:

"Your portfolio is amazing! A personal website could help you attract higher-paying clients..."

E-commerce Sellers

Upgrade marketplace sellers

  • Etsy shops with basic storefronts
  • Amazon sellers using default templates
  • eBay sellers with poor branding
  • Local marketplace vendors

Example Search:

"Etsy shop owner" + "no website"
"Amazon seller looking to expand"

Localized SEO

Find businesses with poor visibility

  • Google "[city] + [service] + no website"
  • Search Yelp for businesses with bad sites
  • Check local directories for basic listings

Example Targets:

"Miami wedding planner no website"
"London handyman site:yelp.com"

Expat Connections

Leverage cultural bridges

  • Kenyan diaspora business groups
  • African entrepreneur networks
  • Local chambers of commerce abroad

Example Approach:

"As a fellow Kenyan, I understand your business needs. Many African businesses abroad benefit from..."

Remote Service Boom

Target location-independent businesses

  • Digital nomad service providers
  • Online coaches and consultants
  • Virtual tour companies
  • International e-learning platforms

Example Targets:

"Virtual assistant services"
"Online business coach" + "need website"
