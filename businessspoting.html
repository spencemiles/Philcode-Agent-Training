7 Key Indicators of a Potential Client

What to look for when evaluating businesses

Digital Absence

No or poor online presence

  • No website or "Under Construction" page
  • Social media with no clear call-to-action
  • Only personal Facebook profile (no business page)
  • Website looks outdated (5+ years old)

Red Flags:

- "Contact us on WhatsApp only"
- Website URL is Facebook page
- Broken links on Google My Business

Manual Processes

Reliance on physical/analog systems

  • Paper receipt books
  • Handwritten inventory lists
  • No digital payment options
  • Appointment book instead of calendar

Spotting Tips:

- Watch for shopkeepers flipping through notebooks
- "No M-Pesa" signs
- Customers waiting while staff search records

Customer Pain Points

Visible frustrations in customer interactions

  • "Do you have a website?" questions
  • Complaints about communication
  • Requests for online ordering
  • "Can I pay digitally?" inquiries

Listening For:

- "How do I know what you have in stock?"
- "Why don't you answer your phone?"
- "I wish I could just order online"

Growth Signs

Businesses scaling beyond current capacity

  • Long customer queues
  • Hiring new staff
  • Expanding product lines
  • Moving to bigger premises

Opportunity:

"Your business is growing fast! A website could help manage increased demand by..."

Competitor Comparison

Others in industry have modernized

  • Similar businesses with websites
  • Competitors offering online services
  • Market leaders using digital tools

Pitch Angle:

"I noticed [Competitor] now offers online booking. Many customers expect this convenience..."

Traditional Marketing

Reliance on outdated advertising

  • Newspaper ads only
  • Flyers with no digital call-to-action
  • Word-of-mouth dependence
  • Billboards without website URLs

Spotting Tips:

- "Visit our shop at..." with no online option
- Posters with just a phone number
- Radio ads with no digital follow-up

Tech Awareness

Showing interest in digital solutions

  • Asking about websites/tech
  • Complaining about inefficiencies
  • Admiring competitors' online presence
  • Young staff pushing for upgrades

Positive Signs:

- "I know we need to get online..."
- "My nephew says we need a website"
- Owner using smartphone for business
