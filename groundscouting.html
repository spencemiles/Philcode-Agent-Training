Ground Scouting Mastery

The art of discovering high-potential businesses in the field

6 Field Scouting Techniques

How to identify and approach businesses in person

The Observer

Spot visual indicators first

  • Handwritten signs instead of printed
  • Outdated marketing materials
  • Long customer queues with manual processes
  • "Call for info" signs with no digital options

Key Targets:

- Shops with busy staff writing orders by hand
- Businesses using paper receipt books
- Places with "No M-Pesa" signs

The Customer

Experience their process firsthand

  • Make a small purchase
  • Ask "Do you have a website?" naturally
  • Note how orders/payments are handled
  • Observe other customers' questions

Example Actions:

- Buy a low-cost item to observe checkout
- Ask "Can I order this online next time?"
- Request business card (check for website)

The Helper

Offer value before pitching

  • Compliment their business first
  • Note a specific challenge you can solve
  • "Have you considered...?" approach

Example Opener:

"Your shop is always busy! Have you thought about taking pre-orders online to reduce queues? I specialize in helping businesses like yours..."

The Networker

Leverage local connections

  • Ask other shop owners for referrals
  • "Who else here could use a website?"
  • Partner with complementary businesses

Example Phrase:

"I'm helping businesses in this area get online. Do you know any owners struggling with [specific challenge]?"

The Trend Spotter

Highlight industry shifts

  • "Many shops like yours now offer..."
  • Point out competitor examples
  • Show customer behavior changes

Example Approach:

"I've noticed 5 shops on this street added online ordering last month. Customers really expect this convenience now."

The Problem Solver

Identify and articulate pains

  • Listen for staff complaints
  • Notice inefficiencies
  • Position as solution, not salesperson

Example Script:

"I overheard you telling customers to call for prices. We could set up instant price checking on a website to save you time."

Prime Scouting Locations

Where to find the highest potential businesses

Market Areas

Stalls with handmade goods, specialty foods, or unique products that could sell online

Service Corridors

Streets with multiple salons, repair shops, or consultants in close proximity

New Developments

Recently opened businesses that may not have established online presence yet

Industrial Areas

Manufacturers and wholesalers who could benefit from direct-to-consumer sales

Ground Approach Scripts

Natural ways to start conversations

Openers
Objections
Closing

Compliment Approach: "I've noticed how busy you are! Have you considered taking online orders to reduce the queue during peak times?"

Problem Observation: "I saw several customers asking about [specific product]. Would a website where people can check availability help?"

Competitor Reference: "Many shops in this area are now letting customers order via WhatsApp. Is that something you'd like to set up?"

"I don't have time": "I completely understand. What if we handled everything and just needed 10 minutes for your approval?"

"My customers don't use websites": "Actually, 78% of Kenyans research local businesses online first. Even if they call after, they'll find you more easily."

"It's too expensive": "Many clients earn back the cost within weeks through new customers. We also offer payment plans - what budget were you thinking?"

Next Step Close: "Great! I'll prepare a free mockup showing exactly what we'd create for you. When are you available to review it?"

Urgency Close: "We have capacity for 2 more clients this month. Would you like me to reserve one spot while we discuss details?"

Alternative Close: "Would you prefer to start with a basic site now, or wait and do a complete package next month?"
